## 6. Regulatory Environment

### Current Framework

| Authority | Jurisdiction | Status |
|-----------|--------------|--------|
| **Israel Securities Authority (ISA)** | Securities tokens | Active oversight |
| **Bank of Israel** | Digital shekel, banking | Research phase |
| **Tax Authority** | Crypto taxation | Property treatment |
| **IMPA** | AML/CFT | VASP requirements |

### Key Regulatory Positions

**Classification:**
- Most digital assets treated as securities (substance over form)
- No dedicated crypto licensing regime (unlike EU MiCAR)
- Securities law applies if token functions as security

**Taxation:**
- Cryptocurrency = Property/Asset (not currency)
- Capital gains: 25% (individuals), corporate rate (businesses)
- Mining/staking: Potentially ordinary income
- VAT: Generally applies to crypto services

**Banking Access:**
- Historically challenging for crypto companies
- Improving through court precedents
- Some banks establishing specialized crypto units
- Key banks: Leumi, Hapoalim, Discount, Mizrahi

### Digital Shekel (Project Shaked)

| Aspect | Status |
|--------|--------|
| Phase | Research/Technical exploration |
| Architecture | Likely DLT-based |
| Privacy focus | High priority |
| Launch date | Not announced |
| International | BIS Innovation Hub collaboration |

### Israel vs. Global Regulatory Comparison

| Aspect | Israel | EU (MiCAR) | US |
|--------|--------|------------|-----|
| Comprehensive Crypto Law | In development | Implemented | Fragmented |
| VASP Licensing | Partial | Full framework | State-by-state |
| Stablecoin Rules | Limited | Detailed | Pending |
| Clarity Level | Medium | High | Low |
