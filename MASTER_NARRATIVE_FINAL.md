### Pain Points by Stakeholder

#### Angels/LPs
- Most don't make real money. 70-90% of investments fail to build. 5-9% launch. Only 1-2% give decent returns. Net: hardly any money. But fund managers make money via management fees.
- Small fund sizes = no access to liquid investments or better venture deals (Pre-ICO)
- Deal flow limited to small network of fund managers
- Investors/LPs have very little say in which deals are invested
- For VC deals: All money sent to projects directly and immediately = total loss if failure
- Very little control on project success

#### Projects
- At mercy of fund managers and their cabal for deal terms
- Post investment, supply is decentralized where funds can decide fate of your chart
- Must run to different vendors for different services (tech, investments, marketing, listing, community building)
- To succeed fast, end up doing deals that kill their tokens and eventually the project
- Good projects with huge potential and don't want to get played don't have many options

#### Ecosystems
- Dependent on fund managers to decide if they want to set up ecosystem fund
- Ecosystems have to provide their own money to set up ecosystem fund with VCs
- Community of ecosystem has hardly any say in ecosystem fund
- High-quality dealflow limited due to team bandwidth and limited presence in regions
- Existing ecosystem projects fail to scale due to lack of expert support and funding beyond grants

#### Service Providers
- **Gated Access / Picking 1,000 Locked Doors:** Getting to right decision maker is real pain. Navigating internal politics is impossible. Spend 6-12++ months to close deal with little/no feedback loop.
- **Projects Discovery Limits:** You only know who you know. Expanding network is time expensive. You might have best service but need to pitch from 0 every time. Keeping up and vetting influx of new builders is demanding.
- Getting in on new meta early is like marrying on blind date. You commit based on recommendation with limited data points, driven by potential returns putting reputation at risk.

## The Platform

### Platform Modules

**Deposit Module:**
Simple module where anyone can connect wallet and make deposit. Choose what type of LP (10% lock or full commit). Choose total commitment over next two years. Get NFT (SHAFT) with all details of investment terms. Multi-wallet backup option. Money goes into our multisig controlled by us.

**Deal Flow Module:**
Anyone can propose deal (member of platform required). 3-5% of total raise goes to proposer. Attach pitch deck, investment details (FDV, min/max check size, vesting schedule, cliff, milestone details). Wallet verification required. DD team contacts project via proposer to verify and negotiate. DD team submits findings on deal page. Community asks questions then votes (1 vote per member). Post simple majority vote, deal goes live for stipulated time for funding. Investor gets NFT (SHAFT) after funding. Project gets NFT as proof of investment.

**Claim Module:**
Anyone needing to claim tokens/funding connects wallet. All valid claims can be redeemed. Secondary sale of SHAFT NFT: new wallet with NFT ownership can claim. For investors: platform checks avg price at token release date and deducts 20% of profit if profit exists. For projects: can claim milestone payment once milestone achievement approved by DAO. If project fails milestone, remaining money returned to investors.

**Governance Module:**
Basic module to vote on milestone and listing of projects or changes to T&C or features of platform. Same proposal module. Voting module.

**Service Module:**
Any project/platform partner can submit proposal for service. Empanelled service providers can bid. Proposer chooses provider. Payment facilitated via claim page. Platform takes % fee from service provider/project. Feedback at end of contract.
